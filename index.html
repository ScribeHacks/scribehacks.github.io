FAQ

What if I am not in high school?

We allow those in middle school to join us, but with added parental permission. If you are in college you are welcome to mentor or give speeches at the hackathon. If you want to be a mentor, you can sign up here or if you want to sponsor you can email us at sponsors@scribehacks.tech

What is the theme?

At ScribeHacks we want people to make something that will help the future instead of the present or past. While this is open ended, we believe that it will spark creativity in each person to think about the future and write the code for it. We believe that by giving a theme that makes you think about the future, you will better plan and develop for it. Whether you want to make an app that will track the energy being used to a robot that can help people who are suffering, we welcome all ideas and hacks that want to make the future of our world a better place.

Who can Participate?

We welcome all middle school and high school students who have an interest in coding and making!

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where participants use their knowledge and skills to create, develop, design, and pitch a solution to a particular problem over 24 hours. They’re open not just to programmers and engineers, but to anyone who’s interested in working together to find answers to the world’s most pressing problems.

What if I have never been to a hackathon before?

No matter what your skills are, we can use your help in ScribeHacks to help solve for a better future. We believe that solutions are better when they integrate diverse ways of thinking. There will be many opportunities to learn new skills or ideas at workshops and from the people around you. We’ll also be holding team building events where you can meet new people. Just come eager to learn and share your own knowledge!

FAQ

What does it cost to attend ScribeHacks?

ScribeHacks is absolutely free! There is no entry fee, ScribeHacks will provide you with plenty of food, snacks, swag, and prizes throughout the 24 hours. We can provide all of this due to our wonderful sponsors!

What should I bring?

Please bring a valid form of identification like a student id or driver's license, a computer (preferably a laptop), chargers, and any hardware you will use for your hack. You can also bring a sleeping bag, pillow, toiletries, and a couple changes of clothes. Food will be provided at the event. No firearms, weapons, alcohol, or illegal drugs are allowed on campus.

What should I do to prepare?

While you are not allowed to start coding or making the art for your project until the hackathon starts, you can always find a team, register for the hacakthon, make mockups in figma, xd, sketch, and get the materials needed to make your awesome project.

Will there be travel reimbursements?

Unfortunately, travel reimbursements cost a lot of our sponsor money and can provide support for only a few people. That is why we cannot offer travel reimbursements this year.

Other questions?

If you have any other questions please ask the hackathon organizer, Soham on Discord (sohamb117#4342) or send us an email at, support@scribehacks.tech
